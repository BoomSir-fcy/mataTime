Metatime content rules

1. Why does Metatime need these rules?

 

Metatime is committed to encouraging users to create and consume high-quality content. But those involve inciting violence, harassment, rumors, deception, and other similar behaviors will not only damage the quality of content, but also weaken the confidence of high-quality content creators, and ultimately shake the whole foundation of the Metatime community. Metatime's content rules are designed to ensure that people can create and consume valuable content safely and freely.

 

2. What content rules does Metatime follow?

Metatime’s content rules include:

A. Safety

Violence: Threats of violence against individuals or groups are not allowed. We also prohibit the praise of violence.

Terrorism/violent extremism: Do not threaten or promote terrorism or violent extremism.

Child molestation: We have a zero tolerance policy for content related to child molestation on Metatime.

Information bullying/harassment: Do not harass or attack others in a targeted manner, or instigate others to do so. This includes hoping that others suffer personal harm.

Hateful behavior: Do not promote violence against others, or threaten or harass others based on their race, ethnicity, nationality, sexual orientation, gender, gender identity, religious beliefs, age, disability or serious illness.

Suicide or self-harm: Do not advocate or encourage suicide or self-harm.

Sensitive content (including bloody violence and adult content): Do not post excessively bloody content, or share content that contains violent or pornographic information.

Illegal or specific controlled goods or services: Metatime services must not be used for any illegal purposes or facilitating any illegal activities. This includes the sale, purchase or facilitation of illegal goods or services, as well as certain types of controlled goods or services.

 

 

B. Privacy

Private information: It is not allowed to post other users' private information (such as home phone number, residential address) without the explicit authorization and permission. We also prohibit behaviors that threaten to disclose others’ private information or encourage others to do so.

Unauthorized nudity: Do not post or share information such as private photos taken or distributed without the consent of the relevant person.

 

C. Authenticity

Platform manipulation and spam: Do not use Metatime's services to artificially boost or suppress information, or participate in manipulating or disrupting users' experience on Metatime.

Fraudulent use: Do not pretend to be other individual, group or organization to plan to or actually mislead, confuse or deceive others.

Copyright and trademarks: Do not infringe on the intellectual property rights of others, including copyrights and trademarks.

 

3. How to implement the above content rules?

A. Who is responsible for maintaining and enforcing these rules?

Metatime is a decentralized content community, its healthy development and operation rely on the support and maintenance of the community. In the early stage, the founding team and volunteers are responsible for dealing with information and accounts that violate content rules; in the long run, we will continue to update the above rules in DAO ways to ensure that the ever-changing online speech scene is considered from a global and diversified perspective. The specific handling of violation information and accounts will also be carried out under the organization of DAO.

 

B. Specific administer standards

We need to find a balance between "allowing content diversity" and "protecting users", so when there is uncertainty about a content violation, we will consider a variety of factors, including (but not limited to):

 

Does the behavior target a specific individual or group?

If a speech is directed at a specific person or a specific group, it is more likely to be defined as a violation.

 

Was the report made by related personnel or bystanders?

The reports made by relevant persons targeted by the remarks will be more likely to be prioritized.

 

Has the user ever violated our rules?

If a person has a history of violations before, his or her alleged violation remarks will be more likely to be defined as a violation.

 

How serious is the violation?

More serious violations will be dealt with first.
